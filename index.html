Process + Intention

Engineering clarity.
Nothing arbitrary.

Every object produced by GIDE begins with a constraint list, not a mood board. Material is chosen for mechanical behaviour first — hardness, grain direction, aging profile — and for appearance second. If a decision cannot be justified by function or longevity, it is removed.

The studio documents every step: from initial problem statements and tolerance studies to prototype iterations and finish selection. That documentation ships with the object. You should know what you are holding and why it is the way it is.

GIDE makes one object at a time. When an object is complete — when it has passed structural review, surface review, and a minimum of three physical prototypes — a new brief is written. There is no catalogue. There is a sequence.
